Question

“Which of the garmin watch single position and the application “my track” gps logger which will be placed on various body locations, provide a more efficient tracking accuracy.”

Introduction

Fitness devices are becoming more popular and constantly changing. Fitbits, Garmin Watches and even our smartphones are popular tools in capturing, analyzing and displaying the results to us. The UI in these devices and applications have become better over time and are very important when it comes to tracking our fitness activities. This website is an interactive report based on a Human computer Interaction (HCI) experiment on the question above, done by AUT students Arshad, Casey, Chris and Zhang. This study aims to find if there is any difference in terms of logging accuracy of a dedicated fitness device (Garmin Watch), and an iPhone 5s with a fitness application installed. We will attempt to see whether the interaction between the Garmin Watch on a person's wrist, or a phone that can be in certain parts of a person’s body is more accurate. The study will consist of an experiment that will record key statistics from the Watch and iPhone application. We will also record the comfort levels a participant feels of both the watch and phone after completing the assessment. This will give us an understanding of where the user would like the device to be kept while exercising and also the accuracy of the devices.

Purpose

The purpose of this experiment is to look at the relationship between the Garmin Watch and an iPhone application. Recording the distance of both devices and the comfort level the user feels after doing the experiment, we hope to find some differences in preference and accuracy. As the phone will be kept in different parts of the body, and the phone remaining constantly on the person’s wrist, we expect there to be differences in both accuracy and comfort level. We will have to design and carry out an experiment that can produce results that can be generalized, allowing for designers to take into account what people like and accuracy for future fitness devices. We hope to draw conclusions from our experiment, so that it can draw out some consideration from designers when designing the human interaction aspect of the next fitness device. This will be done by exploring our data and explaining how it relates to design principles.
