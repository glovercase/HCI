Analysis

In this section we have done some analysis on our data collected in our experiment. We have used Microsoft Excel and RStudio to produce graphs to help us get a better picture of the data collected. We will use the graphs below to see for any trends.

Comparision of Run Data

Red = Garmin

Blue = Iphone5s

Firstly we modelled our distances captured in each test from both the Garmin watch and iPhone application as a barplot. The bar plot shows some differences in the tracking of distances, but not by much. The distances captured from the iPhone always had a minor variance to the figures captured on the Garmin watch. As the graph shows, testers put the phone in various places on their body and travelled at different speeds and different distances. The interference with putting the phone in the backpack, pocket and even the hoodie had no major impact on recording the distance accurately.

Comfort of Iphone

We also recorded the participants comfort levels while using the iPhone. We used a 5 point system, with 5 being the highest and 1 being the lowest. Results were generally favourable with 40% of our participants giving it a score of 4 out of 5. A further 40% indicated that the phone was not an hindrance for them while running with scores of 2 and 3. This shows that in general, running with a phone, and placing with in various places can be comfortable.

Comfort of Garmin

Similarly, we recorded the comfort levels of people using the Garmin watch while running. Using the 5 point system, participants scored the Garmin fairly highly in comfort level. 80% scored the watch either a 5 or 4 on its comfort level. 20% scored it moderately at 2. Overall participants favoured the Garmin, as they didn’t have to worry about it while running.
