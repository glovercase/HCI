Design and Experiment

This test will be designed to compare the comfortability of the Garmin Watch and an application on a phone, and hopefully discover which is more comfortable to users. To perform this experiment, we will get people to do a running exercise with both devices at the same time so accuracy and comfort can be recorded together. We will then compare this data with our hypothesis. The activity we will conduct will involve a person running around a designated track for around 5 minutes. They will carry both the phone and watch and some questions will be asked at the end of the run. Before starting the run, we will ask the users to choose from a set of options that the phone can be placed while on the run. This will include holding it in their hand, in their pocket, in a bag on their back, on a shoulder strap or in front of them in a jacket pocket. After choosing the place to keep their phone, we will then start the device to record the run and ask the participant to start running. They will run around the track for 5 minutes. The Garmin Watch is designed to be used by athletes to track distances and speeds in an outdoor setting (Webber & Porter, 2009, p. 464). With our experiment being done in an outside route at Auckland Domain, we found using the Garmin to be good for our experiment. We used a phone as it can be placed in various places on the body, allowing us to test the comfort of placing it on different parts of the body. For this experiment we chose to use only 5 participants. This is because we will test for the 5 parts of the body that the phone can be kept, giving us data that can be analyzed.

Hypothisis

We hypothesize that the readings on the garmin watch and the “My Track” application on an IPhone 5s will vary due to the track being located in the bush. Yet we believe that the Garmin could be more accurate than the IPhone 5s with “My Track”. Also there may be outliers of data based on connection quality, as the track is also based under trees which can have an effect on the connection of the GPS. Along with the hypothesis that the low amount of tests being done will show that the accuracy of the data may be spread widely. Also each participant will move at different speeds and therefore the distances will be different for each test. With starting both devices it takes a few seconds to set them. The time taken to start and stop the devices will be inconsistent.

List of Steps

  1. Find a willing participant.

  2. Ask the participant to read the terms and conditions of the experiment.

  3. Ask the participant to sign the terms and conditions to take part in the experiment.

  4. Let the participant decide where they would like to put the phone from a list of options provided.

  5. Have the participant wear the Garmin Watch.

  6. Explain to participant what they have to do for this experiment.

  7. Answer any questions participant has.

  8. Set up the app on the Iphone to record the run.

  9. Set up the Garmin on RUN.

  10. Start the watch and the participant starts the test.

  11. At 5min the participant stops.

  12. Remove the devices and track the data.

  13. Ask the participant why they choose that body position.

  14. Ask participant to rate both the phone placement and Garmin placement (on a scale of 1-5).

  15. Thank participant for their time.
